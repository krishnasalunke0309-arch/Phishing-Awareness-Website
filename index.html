What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, bank details and personal data. Attackers often use fake emails, websites and messages that look legitimate.


How Phishing Works

  1. Attacker creates a fake email or website.
  2. User receives the message.
  3. User clicks a malicious link.
  4. Fake website opens.
  5. User enters sensitive information.
  6. Attacker steals the information.

Types of Phishing

1. Email Phishing

The most common phishing attack. Attackers send fake emails pretending to be trusted companies, banks, or services.

Example: "Your account has been suspended. Verify now."

Effects:

  • Password theft
  • Identity theft
  • Financial loss

2. Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific person or organization. Attackers gather personal information before sending the message.

Effects:

  • Data breaches
  • Company information leaks

3. Whaling

A phishing attack that targets high-level executives such as CEOs and managers.

Effects:

  • Large financial losses
  • Business disruption

4. Smishing

Phishing through SMS messages.

Example: "Your package is delayed. Track now."

Effects:

  • Mobile malware
  • Account compromise

5. Vishing

Voice phishing through phone calls.

Example: Fake bank officer requesting OTP.

Effects:

  • Banking fraud
  • OTP theft

6. Clone Phishing

Attackers copy legitimate emails and replace original links with malicious links.

Effects:

  • Credential theft
  • Malware infection

Signs of a Phishing Attack

  • Unknown sender email address
  • Urgent action requests
  • Spelling and grammar mistakes
  • Suspicious website links
  • Unexpected attachments
  • Requests for passwords or OTPs
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers
  • Threatening messages

How to Identify Fake Websites

Check the URL Carefully

Real: https://www.amazon.in

Fake: https://amazon-login-security.xyz

Look for HTTPS

Secure websites usually use HTTPS and display a lock icon.

Check Website Design

Poor-quality images, broken links, and bad formatting can indicate a fake website.

Verify Domain Name

Always check spelling carefully.

Real: facebook.com

Fake: faceboook.com

Effects of Phishing

1. Financial Loss

Attackers can steal banking information and transfer money from victim accounts.

2. Identity Theft

Personal information can be used to impersonate the victim.

3. Data Breach

Sensitive company or personal information may be exposed.

4. Malware Infection

Victims may unknowingly install malware, ransomware, or spyware.

5. Reputation Damage

Organizations can lose customer trust after phishing incidents.

Prevention Methods

  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Verify sender identity before responding.
  • Never share OTPs or passwords.
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links.
  • Keep software and browsers updated.
  • Use trusted antivirus software.
  • Check URLs before entering credentials.
  • Report suspicious emails.
  • Stay informed about cyber threats.

Real World Phishing Examples

Google Docs Phishing Attack (2017)

Millions of Gmail users received fake Google Docs invitations. Users unknowingly granted attackers access to their accounts.

Impact

  • Thousands of accounts affected
  • User data exposed
  • Global awareness increased

Facebook and Google Scam

Attackers sent fake invoices and payment requests, causing companies to lose millions of dollars.

Impact

  • Huge financial losses
  • Business disruption

Conclusion

Phishing is one of the most common cyber threats in today's digital world. Cybercriminals use fake emails, websites, messages and phone calls to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

By understanding phishing techniques, recognizing warning signs, using strong passwords, enabling Two-Factor Authentication and staying alert online, users can protect themselves from cyber attacks.

Cybersecurity awareness is the first step toward a safer internet experience.
